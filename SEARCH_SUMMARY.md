## How These Papers Support Your Hypothesis

### Evidence Chain:

1. **Geometry-Dependent Conduction** (Kucera 1998)
   - Conduction velocity depends on tissue geometry and branching
   - Reduced load → increased conduction velocity
   - Foundation for boundary effects

2. **Electrotonic Loading Effects** (Connolly 2015)
   - **Direct evidence** that electrotonic load modulates safety factor
   - Load gradients exist at boundaries
   - Load reduction → increased excitability

3. **Heterogeneous Coupling at Boundaries** (Kucera 2014)
   - **Direct evidence** of different conduction at boundary transitions
   - Nonlinear effects at heterogeneous regions
   - Demonstrates boundary effects experimentally

4. **Reduced Load from Inert Tissue** (Multiple papers)
   - Inert tissue provides no electrotonic load
   - Should increase safety factor compared to uniform tissue
   - Leads to increased conduction velocity

5. **Gap Junction Organization at Boundaries** (Shaw, Smyth, et al.)
   - Gap junctions are actively organized at boundaries
   - Allows fine control of coupling at specific locations
   - Supports role of boundary organization in function

6. **Recent Comprehensive Review** (Kléber 2021)
   - **Validates entire research framework**
   - States explicitly that coupling and structure determine conduction
   - Discusses safety factor and boundary effects

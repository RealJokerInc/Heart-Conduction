  Verdict

  The separable spectral approach вҖ” DCT along Neumann axes, DST along Dirichlet
  axes вҖ” produces solutions identical to PCG (within solver tolerance) on the
  same FDM system. The key insight is that the 2D Laplacian eigenvalues are
  additive: О»_{i,j} = D(О»_x_i + О»_y_j), so mixing transform types per-axis is
  mathematically exact, not an approximation.

  This means bath_coupled_edges([TOP, BOTTOM]) gets the O(N log N) spectral
  solver instead of iterative PCG вҖ” no accuracy loss, major speed gain. The
  auto-selector now correctly picks spectral for any per-axis uniform BC
  configuration, only falling back to PCG when opposite edges on the same axis
  have different BC types (which can't happen with physical bath
  configurations).

KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. LITERATURE DISCOVERY
   ✓ Active research field: Belmiloudi et al. (2015-2019) have already
     developed coupled LBM approaches for cardiac bidomain with published
     papers in Springer and Mathematical Modelling of Natural Phenomena
   ✓ Clinical precedent: LBM-EP demonstrates 10-45× speedup over FEM
     for monodomain cardiac simulations
   ✓ Theoretical framework: Chapman-Enskog theory supports LBM for
     coupled parabolic-elliptic systems

2. ARCHITECTURE ANALYSIS
   ✓ RECOMMENDED: Architecture A (Dual-Lattice LBM)
     - Two independent LBM lattices (one per field)
     - Coupled through source terms
     - Maintains pure LBM framework and parallelization
     - Literature validated
   
   ~ Architecture B (Hybrid LBM-Classical)
     - LBM for parabolic Vm, classical solver for elliptic φe
     - Conservative approach, slower but proven
     - Use if pseudo-time convergence is poor
   
   ✗ Architecture C (Single Enlarged)
     - Not recommended: breaks physics, numerically unstable

3. FEASIBILITY ASSESSMENT
   ✓ Theoretically: HIGH
     - LBM for parabolic equations: proven (existing monodomain)
     - LBM for elliptic equations: feasible via steady-state (literature)
     - Multi-field coupling: standard practice (multi-phase flows)
     - Anisotropic conductivity: MRT collision handles (separate τ)
   
   ✓ Computationally: HIGH
     - Memory: ~2 GB GPU (fits V100 easily)
     - Parallelization: straightforward (LBM is embarrassingly parallel)
     - Data movement: minimal, not a bottleneck
   
   ~ Practically: MEDIUM-HIGH
     - Unknown: pseudo-time convergence rate for cardiac bidomain
     - Risk: Time step restrictions and coupling stability
     - Mitigation: Phase 1 proof-of-concept first

4. PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS
   
   Monodomain LBM:    1-10 ms/step     (baseline, proven)
   Bidomain LBM opt:  5-20 ms/step     (if pseudo-time converges well)
   Bidomain LBM cons: 50-200 ms/step   (if many sub-iterations needed)
   FEM Bidomain:      100-1000 ms/step (current gold standard)
   
   Speedup vs. FEM:   2-50× depending on elliptic solver convergence
   Expected:          5-20× is realistic target

5. CRITICAL UNKNOWN (Risk Factor)
   
   QUESTION: How many pseudo-time iterations needed per physical step?
   
   - Literature suggests: 50-200 iterations
   - With multigrid: possibly reduced to 10-50
   - Impact: Determines if Architecture A is viable
   
   Mitigation: Phase 1 empirical validation on simple benchmarks

TECHNICAL HIGHLIGHTS
================================================================================

BIDOMAIN FORMULATION:
  V_m equation (parabolic):
    χ·Cm·∂Vm/∂t + χ·Iion = ∇·(Di·∇Vm) + ∇·(Di·∇φe)
  
  φe equation (elliptic):
    0 = ∇·(Di·∇Vm) + ∇·((Di+De)·∇φe)

LBM SOLUTION STRATEGY:
  ├─ Lattice 1 (V_m): τ_m = 0.5 + 3·D_i·dt/dx²
  ├─ Lattice 2 (φe): τ_e = 0.5 + 3·(D_i+D_e)·dt/dx²
  └─ Coupling: Source terms linking the two lattices

MEMORY REQUIREMENTS:
  Monodomain:  0.94 GB (single distribution function)
  Bidomain:    1.88 GB (two distribution functions)
  Multigrid:   + 0.5-1 GB per coarse level

GPU COMPATIBILITY:
  ✓ D3Q7 lattice (7 velocities, minimal memory)
  ✓ MRT collision (multiple relaxation times for anisotropy)
  ✓ PyTorch/CUDA ready (streaming via torch.roll)
  ✓ Scales to multi-GPU (no special coupling needed)

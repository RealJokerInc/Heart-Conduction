QUICK FACT REFERENCE
================================================================================

Conduction Velocity Facts:
- Normal ventricular conduction: ~0.3-0.4 m/s
- Purkinje fibers (fast): ~2-4 m/s
- Tissue with reduced coupling: ~0.05-0.1 m/s
- At boundaries: Changes based on load asymmetry

Safety Factor Facts:
- Safety factor = (V_max × τ) / Threshold
- V_max = maximum rate of depolarization
- τ = membrane time constant
- Higher safety factor = faster conduction
- Reduced load → higher safety factor

Electrotonic Load Facts:
- Caused by current flow to passive tissue
- Reduces V_max during propagation
- Gradient in load creates gradient in safety factor
- At boundary with inert tissue: one-sided load reduction

Gap Junction Facts:
- Connexin 43 (Cx43) is main cardiac connexin
- Forms low-resistance channels between cells
- Voltage-dependent gating
- ~40% of intercalated disc is gap junctions
- Can be dynamically regulated

## Key Themes in Related Papers

### 1. Voltage-Dependent Properties
- Gap junctions have voltage-dependent gating properties
- These properties vary by connexin type and temperature
- Affects tissue-level conduction characteristics

### 2. Channel Clustering and Organization
- Ions channels and gap junctions are organized into clusters
- Clustering affects ephaptic coupling
- Organization at boundaries is dynamically controlled

### 3. Multi-Scale Modeling
- Computational approaches must integrate multiple scales
- Single-cell properties determine tissue behavior
- Boundary conditions critically affect simulations

### 4. Scar and Remodeled Tissue
- Multiple mechanisms alter conduction at scar boundaries
- Both structural (geometry) and electrical (coupling) factors matter
- Gap junction remodeling is a key mechanism

### 5. Genetic and Developmental Control
- Conduction velocity is under developmental genetic control
- Connexin expression levels determine conduction properties
- Tissue-specific differences are genetically determined

## How to Access These Papers

### Primary Method: PubMed
1. Go to pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov
2. Search by PMID directly
3. Most papers provide links to full-text PDFs

### University/Institutional Access
- If you have institutional affiliation, many papers are available through library access
- Use your institution's proxy or VPN

### Open Access Options
- Check for PMC ID (PubMed Central) - these are freely available
- Check for DOI links to publisher - some offer free access

### Author Direct Request
- Many authors provide PDFs upon request
- Reach out to corresponding authors
